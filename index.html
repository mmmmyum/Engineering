1/12/24 - This is my eleventh journal entry

  1. our first bridge weighed 41.7 grams. our final bridge weighed 54 grams.
  2. our first bridge carried 220 grams. our final bridge carried 1623 grams
  3. the ratio of weight carried to the weight of the first bridge was 5.3. the ratio for our final bridge was 30.1
  4. for our first bridge, the majority of the weight was distributed on the center of it. this is because we had a supporting structure in the middle for both of the ends. for our second bridge, the weight was pretty evenly distributed. id say near the ends of the bridge was where most of the weight was. our second bridge also largely consisted of glue so that was a major component to the weight.
  5. the design of our first bridge took inspiration from the golden gate bridge. we replicated the supporting structure on it and put it onto our bridge. our second bridge didn't really take inspiration from anything, we just kind of came up with it.
  6. the first bridge we made had an unsuccessful conclusion, since it only held 8x it's weight. before we tested it, i accidentally broke it a little when picking it up off the paper. our 2nd design worked pretty well though.
  7. the point of failure for our first bridge was unexpected, since we expected it to be in the middle. however, when we tested it, it was actually somewhat near the ends. for our 2nd bridge though, the point of failure was in the middle which was expected.
  8. if our first bridge had more support on the bottom, it would've been much more successful. we focused too much on trying to improve the insignificant parts of the bridge. however, our 2nd bridge design could've easily been fixed if we spread out the noodles more evenly. the way they were arranged was very disorganized and there were way too many spaces between the noodles than needed.
